Frequently Asked Questions

How long are the projects?

Projects are 5 weeks long. They may continue after that as we intend to take some of the projects in to next year science and other competitions. We may have to assign new mentors after summer, during regular year. We have not reserved space at libraries for the next year yet.

How are the projects done?

Typical project has 5 students, preferably a mix of grades. Each group has 1 or 2 mentors who are professionals in the relevant field. Mentors and students meet once a week at least at a reserved time/space in the local library/school. They may make their own arrangements to meet more often. TheOpClub leader assigned to the group can help with logistics. Initial project proposals are high level definitions. Creating project proposal itself is part of the project to learn all aspects of doing projects, Students work on the projects on their own and use the meeting time with mentors for planning, design, hands on learning etc. There will work shops conducted on project management, innovation etc. to help teach how to manage projects.

Where are the Mentor meetings held?

At this time, we have two confirmed locations. One in BASIS independent silicon valley school, downtown San Jose and in Village square library in Evergreen, San Jose. We are working on another location in Fremont. Parents can help create more locations by volunteering.

What is the time commitment?

We intend the projects to be rigorous and what you get is what you put in. Scheduled in person mentoring time is 2 to 8 hours per week. Typical project will require 4 hours to 8 hours per day of work for 5 weeks. Additional time is required to keep in touch with mentors over e-mails.

Can I commit to summer projects only

Some projects may be further refined after 5 weeks and continued in to next year for entering in to competitions. This may require additional time commitment at the beginning of the year. You may choose to drop out after the summer and let other team members interested in continuing to pursue the project.

As a Mentor what day/time am I expected to help?

We reserved space Every Tuesday 10AM-12Noon at the Village square library. We are in the process of reserving on Thursday and Saturday. We may start other time slots based on availability of mentors. We have not blocked any time at other locations in Fremont and BASIS, downtown San Jose. We aim to provide up to 8 hours of in person mentoring time per week for each project. You may start with as little of 2 hours per week in person. TheOpClub leader assigned to your project can help facilitate different times convenient to every one in the project.
